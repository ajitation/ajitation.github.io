Hamilton Studios
Publication Design for the oldest photography studio in Mumbai.


Hamilton studios in Mumbai (erstwhile Bombay) is famous for being the oldest surviving photography studio in the city.

Started in 1928, Hamilton studio has preserved its distinct style of studio photography even in the digital age. I designed a publication documenting the craft of photography as they practice it. NID Garba 2017
Visual Identity for the Garba celebration at NID


Garba is the name of a folk dance with many forms performed during the Indian festival of Navratri. Originating from the state of Gujarat, the word “Garba” is derived from the sanskrit word “Garbha” which means womb.

During the nine nights of Navratri, the dance is performed in a circle as it symbolises time and its cycles. The rings of dancers revolve in concentric circles around a shrine of the Goddess placed in the center. Being students at NID who are passionate about Garba, my friend Chinmayee and I took on this project of giving NID’s Garba a new visual identity for the year of 2017.


The idea was to create a dynamic and modular identity system by mapping the footwork in different syles of Garba.



For example, here is the basic unit of 12-step garba repeated around a circle.


An identity reveal video to showcase the nine sub-logos: Since the festival lasts nine days, we decided to derive nine sub-logos from the mapped footwork and colour them with a gradient, the prominent colours being picked from the colours traditionally associated with that day.

A scene from this year's garba: The modular idenity system with the logos fitting concentrically is a reference to the growing and shrinking circles of people dancing.


The two primary logos



Further, a system of nine sub-logos was made for each day of the festival. These nine logos fit concentrically around the main logo just the way the dancing circles of people do.



Applications of the identity The identity was used in the following contexts: e-mail invites, banners, stamps, posters, light projections.





As an environmental application, we made projection units with stencils that projected the logos on the path towards the garba field.

KahaNID
Narrative & Merchandise Design for the NID building.


The objective of this project was to come up with a narrative system that enabled visitors to the NID building to understand the institutes history through its architecture.



The proposed solution involved a treasure hunt with a slideshow camera and a leaflet as clues. The different stories of NID were told through encrypted posters placed across the building.






The objective of the game is to find the right poster by matching the images in the camera to the right poster. By placing the leaflet on the right poster, the story bits on the leaflet becomes readable. Thus, the story gets completed by finding all the posters across campus.

Quarterly Typeface Calendar
A typographic calendar for the year 2017.


This calendar was designed on the occasion of the 37th Convocation ceremony at NID. It is a foldable calendar divided into quarters. Each quarter has a typeface from a distinct type family - Modernist, Humanist, Geometric and Calligraphic. The weight of the typeface increases every month. The size of the unfolded calendar is smaller than an A3.

The Data Diet
Visualising trends in food color through instagram images


As part of our data visualisation course, my team and I decided visualise trends in food color and popular cuisine categories across different cities of India.

For our sample set of food images, we downloaded 10 instagram images from 10 restaurants in 5 cities of India. This gave us a total sample size of 500 images.




Next, we used an algorithm to pick out dominant colors from all the images.


Then, all the dominant colors and their hexcodes were arranged in an excel sheet and sorted by restaurant, number of likes and cities.




And lastly, this data was visualised in a customised form of the Nightingale Graph. The five sectors of the circle represent the colour groups formed by cuisine categories. The size of the inner sections represents the frequency of food items from that cuisine category. The gradients in the sections are average colours found in that cuisine in the region.


Matchbox 2.0
Ergonomic redesign of a matchbox


For this two-week long packaging project, I decided to reimagine the matchbox and reduce the effort required in striking a match. Along with this, later in the project, I also modified the opening of the matchbox to create a dispensing mechanism that lets you shake out only 1-2 matches at a time. This prevents accidental dropping of excess matchsticks in the dark.
